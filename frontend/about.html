About Dementia

A short, readable primer for judges and users which is concise, accurate, and educational. This page is for information only.

What is Dementia?

Dementia caused by neurodegeneration in motor regions of brain which collectively account for over 50% of dementia cases worldwide, include Frontotemporal Dementia (FTD), Dementia with Lewy Bodies (DLB), and Motor Neuron associated dementias after Dementia caused by Alzheimer’s disease. These conditions often involve progressive degeneration of the brain’s motor regions, which control voluntary movements and speech production, leading to impairments such as slurred speech, monotone pitch, and reduced volume. Our AI-assisted dementia screening tool leverages voice biomarkers analyzing acoustic features like jitter, shimmer, and harmonic-to-noise ratio alongside cognitive assessments evaluating memory, attention, and executive function, to detect subtle early signs of neurodegeneration.

Why voice?

Early neurodegenerative changes often manifest in speech and behavior. Acoustic biomarkers—such as pitch variability (jitter), amplitude variability (shimmer), and harmonic-to-noise ratio—can capture these early changes in speech patterns. When combined with cognitive quizzes assessing memory, attention, and executive function, these features allow AI-assisted systems to detect early signs of dementia caused by motor-region degeneration. sleep disturbances, smell loss, and changes in speech and voice.

What this demo does

  • Uses a small public dataset (Kaggle) of recorded voice-derived features.
  • Shows a simple machine learning model (Decision Tree) trained for educational demonstration.
  • Returns an educational prediction and a brief feature-wise explanation not a medical diagnosis.

Limitations & ethics

This web demo is not a medical device. It uses a limited dataset and a simple model. It cannot replace clinical evaluation by neurologists. Inputs are numeric features, not raw audio, and no personal data is stored by default.

Key voice features we use

  • Fo / Fhi / Flo — fundamental frequency (pitch averages & range)
  • Jitter — pitch instability (cycle-to-cycle)
  • Shimmer — amplitude instability
  • HNR — harmonic-to-noise (clarity)
  • DFA — complexity / long-range correlation

Quick QA for judges

How does the demo work?

Frontend posts 7 numeric features to a Flask API (/predict). Backend scales the numbers and uses a saved model to predict. The UI shows prediction + a feature-level note.

Is this accurate?

Not for clinical use. Small dataset and a simple model suitable for learning and prototype demonstrations only.

Privacy

No audio or personal data stored by default. Inputs are numeric features only.
